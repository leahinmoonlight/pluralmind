# Adding a Slime2 Chat Widget with Pluralmind Support

Slime2 supports Pluralmind out-of-the-box, and it's even enabled by default. All you actually need to do is set up a Slime2 Chat Box, and proxied messages will just work~!

::: tip Prefer to watch how it's done?
We also have a [video tutorial](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=spZdrFe5P18) that goes through all the steps.
:::

## Slime2 Chat Widget Setup

1. [Download and install Slime2](https://zaytri.itch.io/slime2).
2. Open Slime2, and go to the pink **Accounts** tab.
3. Click **Add Account**, and choose **Read Account**. Follow the prompts to connect your Twitch account, and hit **Save** when you're done.
4. Go to the green **Widgets** tab.
5. Click on a blank slime, then choose **Install New Widget**, and **Chat Box** to create a new chat widget.
6. In the top-right, click **Overlay URL** and then click the green **Copy** button.
7. In OBS, add a new **Browser Source** to your scene. It'll ask for a name, feel free to call it something like Slime2.
8. Clear out the existing URL and paste in the one you copied from Slime2. Click **OK** to finish adding the widget. Don't worry if it's small at first, you can always resize it.

That's it, you now have an on-stream chat widget with Pluralmind support!

You can also go back to Slime2 to customize the chat widget's styling, and change a bunch of other settings!
